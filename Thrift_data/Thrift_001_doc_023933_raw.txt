Image: /ttmbforum/images/icons/sheepy2.gif Galveston Jetties on Good Friday



WE put in at Texas City Dike at 8am. Headed over to the North Jetty. Sat there for about an hour in a
severe chop and caught nothing but a single gafftop. (although my one buddy did get some severe
sea sickness)



Cruised over to the S. Jetty, and anchored about half way down(close to one of the party boats)
and about 50 yards off the jetty. We threw out lines with live shrimp, cut shad and fresh dead
crab.



Nothing touched the crab all day. The shrimp landed us 2 bull reds which were fun to pull on with
light tackle.



The cut shad was the food of choice that day. We pulled in 6 more bull reds with it, and also got in 2
yellow fin tuna. I had never caught tuna before, and didnt know that they would be there at the
jetty, but man were they fun to fight. They really grabbed the line and started spooling it out.
Thank goodness it was on my big pole with 50lb test and a steel leader on it. One weighed in at
27lbs and the other at 38lbs.



I am trying to get the picture off my phone to post them. They made some great fillets, afterI
figured out how to clean them.



tightlines all!

( Linnaeus , 1766 ) Crevalle jack , Caranx hippos , is a fast, saltwater fish that can be found in
inland waters along the shoreline of the western Atlantic Ocean from Nova Scotia to Uruguay
and the eastern Atlantic from Portugal to Angola . It has a large rounded head with large eyes
and a dark silvery body that can show hints of blue-green to green-gold. They grow to more than
three feet in length, though more commonly they are between one and two and a half feet long. The
fish usually weighs between three and five pounds, but a 51 pound Crevalle Jack was taken off
the coast of Florida . Crevalle jacks can be poisonous to eat due to the threat of ciguatera
poisoning, but they are prized as a game fish.

Crevalle jacks in a reef in the Caribbean Sea.





Crevalle jacks spawn offshore from early March to early September. When young, they run in
large schools, but become solitary as they get older. They are preyed upon by many surface
feeding carnivores, including finfish , such as the striped marlin , and seabirds. Crevalle
jacks feed during the day and eat a variety of fish and invertebrates . Other common names for
Caranx hippos include common jack, blacktailed trevally, cabalo, green jack, horse
mackerel, horse-eye jack, kingfish, and trevally.
