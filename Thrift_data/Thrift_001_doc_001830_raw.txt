No stimulants = sleep

By jeg700 on April 6, 2012

Yesterday was my first day without taking a stimulant , which was Dexedrine . Â What a
difference!!!

Image: http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/12/Dexedrine_doj.jpg

I had just enough time to get home after 4 hours of work , before I crashed. Â Fell onto the
couch with my little Boo and we slept for 3 hours!

Then, last night, I went to bed at 10pm, forcing myself to stay up that long.

7 hours later, I’m up.

Sleep Tight (film) (Photo credit: Wikipedia)

7 hours people!

7 long beautiful hours of deep peaceful restful sleep:)

I lived decades without drug treatment for my ADHD , and I can continue to do so in the future,
only better.

Why better?

Because I am more mature (smirk) now, well-informed and well aware.

Taking medication to treat ADHD only makes me aware of all my quirks which annoy me because I
tend to try to fix them. Â Prevented sleep. Â Made eating all but impossible! Â I
frustrate myself needlessly by trying to be “normal”. Â I tried to be normal because it
was annoying to constantly lose stuff, forget stuff and mess up all the time. Â Annoying
myself all the livelong day made me angry. Â Angry made me less tolerant of others.
Â Especially the doctor platitude, with his endless questions about why I was feeling all
that anger . Â He made me anxious with his attitude because I would go away feeling as though I
wasn’t trying hard enough.



Image: http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3278/5862525812_c421b8829a_m.jpg

I will give these medications a fighting chance to “work” as they say in the medical field.
Â Strattera takes about 2 weeks to kick in but once they do the effects are supposed to last 24
hours a day , not wear off but provide a continuous supply of norepinephrine, in a sense
(actually, it just provides stimulation to that part of the brain) which allows better
concentration.

We will see. Â The side effects are like all the rest, disrupted sleep, appetite
suppressed, etc. Â I refuse to live with that kind of disruption to my natural self.

I often think that the acceptance of disrupted sleep is contra intuitive to the end results.
Â Without sleep, everything deteriorates drastically, including the ability to think
straight.

Seems to me, anything that prevents the body’s ability to repair and restore itself cannot
be as effective as they would have everyone believe.
