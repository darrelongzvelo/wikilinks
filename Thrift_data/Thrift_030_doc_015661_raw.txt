A highly decorated Gulf War fighter pilot who had trained at the elite United States Navy
Strike Fighter Tactics Instructor program (SFTI) popularly known as TOPGUN , died when his
1977 twin-engine Ted Smith Aerostar 601P light aircraft, registration N700PS , crashed on
Monday, January 16, 2012 at 12:40 p.m. EST shortly after taking off from Philadelphia
Municipal Airport (MPE) in Neshoba County , Mississippi , as reported on Tuesday, January 17,
2012 by Kathryn Aviation News , The Meridian Star , the Aviation Safety Network , WTOK-TV and
other media sources.

The 48-year-old pilot, Richard Harper Howarth, Jr. , of Meridian, MS had flown 44 combat
missions in McDonnell Douglas F/A-18 Hornet supersonic fighters during Operation Desert
Storm in the battle to liberate Kuwait from occupation by Iraq , as part of the coalition force
from 34 nations led by the United States, and authorized by the United Nations . View
slideshow: Former Navy Top Gun pilot dies in Mississippi Aerostar crash

In those military actions he was awarded two Air Medals and two Navy Commendation medals with
â€œVâ€� for valor in combat, and had also finished first in his flight training
class as well as in his officer commissioning class, receiving the Distinguished Naval
Graduate Award .

Mr. Howarth had also briefly flown for ValuJet Airlines (J7), and had worked at Federal
Express for 16 years , since 1995 , where he was a flight instructor and developed the company's
MD-11 pilot training program.

According to his sister, Mrs. Sarah Howarth Marshall , who contacted us about this tragedy,
Richard was obsessed with aviation safety.

He also founded HD Machines, LLC , which offers mechanical and chemical vegetation
management services for the railroad sector, clearing road crossings.

An attached video clip and slide show also accompany this report, and includes new photos
added on Thursday, January 26, 2012 , received from the Howarth family..

Friends and family of the aviator considered Mr. Howarth as a multi-talented, caring
Renaissance man , who was always striving for excellence in all his relationships and
endeavors.

Services for Richard Harper Howarth, Jr. will be held n Saturday, January 21, 2012 , at 2:00
p.m. at Saint Paulâ€™s Episcopal Church . Reverend J. Brian Ponder will preside over the
memorial service with burial following it at the nearby Magnolia Cemetery .

The National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB) is investigating the fatal crash in which
the Aerostar 601P impacted the ground about 900 feet east of the 5,001 foot runway.

The Ted Smith Aerostar became known as the Piper Aerostar in 1978 when the company was acquired
by the Piper Aircraft Corporation . A total of 492 planes of the 601P designation were built, a
pressurized version of the model 601 .

The Aerostar 601P has a maximum speed of 306 mph , a cruising speed of 242 mph , a range of 1,024
miles , a service ceiling of 25,000 feet , and a rate of climb of 1,840 feet per minute .

According to accident records collected by the Aviation Safety Network , there have been a
total of 161 incidents involving this aircraft, many of them fatal accidents from September
15, 1969 to the present.

We extend our heartfelt condolences to Mr. Howarth's family, friends, and associates over
this tragic and untimely loss.
