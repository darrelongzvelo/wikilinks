torii gates



A torii is a traditional Japanese gate commonly found at the entry to a Shinto shrine , although
it can be found at Buddhist temples as well, such as at Hase-dera in Kamakura .

The basic structure of a torii is two columns that are topped with a horizontal rail called the
kasagi . Slightly below the top rail is a second horizontal rail called the nuki . Torii are
traditionally made from wood and are frequently painted vermilion . When unbarked logs are
used for the torii it is called a kuroki , or "black wood" torii. Today, torii made of stone,
metal or stainless steel can be found as well.

Torii mark the transition from the sacred (the shrine) to the profane (the normal world) (see
Sacred-profane dichotomy ) . Inari shrines typically have many torii. A person who has been
successful in business often donates a torii in gratitude. The Fushimi Inari shrine in Kyoto
has thousands of such torii.

One type of torii gate is the Shimmei-style. In a Shimmei-style torii the kasagi bar is a round
log that juts out over the edges of the two supporting columns while the nuki bar simply
connects the them. One example of a Shimmei-style torii is the torii that is located outside of
Emperor ShÅ�wa 's gravesite. Another type of torii is the Ise-style, where the nuki is the
same as the Shimmei but the kasagi is not round, but pentagonal-shaped. The most popular, and
modern, style of torii is the Myojin-style. Myojin-style torii are built with curved kasagi
rails that sit on top of a secondary upper bar called the shimaki . There is also a vertical beam
called the gakuzuka which connects the shimaki to the nuki . The gakazuka is often the location
where a shrine will hang a tablet with its name. The forth type of torii is the Ryoubu-style,
which is also known as yotsuashi , or the "four-legged style". This type of torii is similar to
the Myojin-style except it has four supporting posts that surround the two columns. The
"floating torii" at the Itsukushima Shrine is a good example of a Ryoubu-style torii. Torii
usually are not equipped with any doors, although there are a small percentage that do have
doors, such as those at Ise Shrine , Kasuga Shrine , and ÅŒmiwa Shrine.

The magnificient and the largest temple ever, Candi Borobudur.



So, what so special about this temple?( FAQ )



THE HISTORY



Candi Borobudur is the biggest temple in Indonesia which is located in Magelang, Central
Java, in addition to tourism to be a busy visit, also a center for Buddhist worship in
Indonesia, especially in the celebration each Waisak. This is consistent with the meaning of
the name which is "a monastery in the hills." Borobudur is currently defined as a UNESCO World
Heritage.



Borobudur was built around 800 BC or 9th century. Borobudur was built by the Mahayana Buddhist
religion in the government Sailendra dynasty. This temple was built in the heyday Sailendra
dynasty. Borobudur founded by the King who came from Samaratungga or Sailendra dynasty.
Likelihood of this temple was built around 824 M and finished around the year 900 BC-a period in
which the Queen is the daughter Pramudawardhani from Samaratungga. While the architect who
built this temple meritorious according to the story called hereditary Gunadharma.



Borobudur is the name meaning "monastery in the hills," which comes from the word "bara"
(temple or monastery) and "beduhur" (the hills or high) in Sanskrit. Therefore, in
accordance with the meaning of the name Borobudur, then this place since first used as a place
of Buddhist worship.



Image:
http://3.bp.blogspot.com/_vurYco2yS-M/SlxJ9jzyMMI/AAAAAAAAAAk/acHm29JYSCE/s320/sir-thomas-raffles.jpg

Borobudur own words based on the written evidence that the first written by

Sir Thomas Stamford Raffles , Governor General of the United Kingdom in

Java, which gives the name of this temple. The only document that indicates the existence of
the oldest temple is Nagarakretagama book, written by mpu Prapanca in the year 1365. In the
book is written that this temple is used as a place of Buddhist meditation.



This temple for centuries are no longer used. Then, as volcanic eruptions,

most of the Borobudur Temple building is closed. In addition,

the building also coverde by various trees and shrubbery for centuries.



In the year 1814 when the British occupied India, Sir Thomas Stamford Raffles hear the
findings of ancient huge Bumisegoro area in the village of Magelang. Because the interest of
the history of Java, Raffles then immediately ordered the HC Cornelius, a Dutch engineer, to
investigate the location of the discovery of a hill when it met the bush.

Cornelius assisted by about 200 men to down the cut trees and remove the shrub that covered the
giant building. Because the building is already considering fragile and can fall apart, then
to Cornelius report his findings to Raffles including some pictures. Because of the
invention Raffles received an award as the person who started restoration of Borobudur
temple and get the attention of the world. In the year 1835, the entire temple area has been
successfully dug. The

restoration continue as Holland arrived to indonesia.



After Indonesia gained its independence, in 1956, the Indonesian government to request
assistance of UNESCO to examine damage to Borobudur. Then in 1963, out official decision by
the Indonesian government to do the Borobudur restoration with assistance from UNESCO.
However, this new restoration really began to be done on 10 August 1973. New restoration
process is completed in 1984. Since 1991, Borobudur temple established as World Heritage or
World Heritage Site by UNESCO.

CANDI BOROBUDUR



Borobudur temple is located in Magelang, Central Java, about 40 km from Yogyakarta.
Borobudur has 10 levels and consists of 6 square-shaped levels, 3 levels of circular-shaped
coil and a stupa as a main peak. Di setiap tingkat terdapat beberapa stupa. At every level there
are some stupa. 72 stupa there is a whole other than the main stupa. In each there is a statue of a
Buddhist stupa. Ten levels of Buddhist philosophy which describes the ten Bodhisattva
levels should be to achieve perfection in a Buddhist Nirwana. This perfection symbolized by
the main stupa at the top level. Borobudur structure when viewed from above form the structure
of the mandala that Buddhist cosmology and describes the way people think.





Image:
http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_vurYco2yS-M/SlxKh4-AcMI/AAAAAAAAAAs/XkRf_ZfnubY/s320/borobudur.gif



On the fourth side there is a temple gate and a ladder to the level above it like a pyramid. This
describes the Buddhist philosophy that is all life comes from the stones. The stone became
sand, and a plant, and insects to be, and then become wild animals, and pets, and became the last
man. This process is known as reincarnation. The process is to become the last of the soul and
eventually into Nirwana. Each stage in the process of enlightenment of life is based on
Buddhist philosophy and is depicted on the relief sculpture on the whole temple of Borobudur.

This giant building is only a giant pile of stone blocks that have a height of 42 meters total.
Each stone through without using cement or adhesive. Stones is only based on the pattern and
through stacked. The bottom of the Borobudur temple measuring 118 m around each of the side.
Stones used as approximately 55,000 cubic meters. All stone is taken from rivers around the
Borobudur Temple. These stones cut and transported ago through a pattern such as a game
release. All without using glue or cement.

Meanwhile, relief began to be made after the stones are stacked and finished to. Relief is
available on the temple walls. Borobudur Temple in 2670 has a different relief. Relief is read
clockwise direction round. This relief depicts a story of how it begins and ends at the gate in
the east. This shows that the main gate to the east of the Borobudur temple as generally other
Buddhist. CANDI BOROBUDUR TODAY(CELEBRATION AND TOUR)



Each year at the full moon in the month of May (or June on the leap year), the Buddha in the temple
of Borobudur in Waisak. Waisak generally known as birth, death and the moment when Siddharta
Gautama obtain the highest wisdom to become the Buddha Shakyamuni. The third event is called
the Trisuci Waisak. Waisak ceremony focused on three Buddhist temples by walking from temple
to temple Mendut Pawon and ends at Borobudur.

Waisak at night, especially when the second-peak of the second full moon, Buddhist gathered
around Borobudur. At that time, Borobudur is held as a gathering place of supernatural
strength. According to belief, at the time Waisak, the Buddha will appear at the top of the
mountain visible in the south. At this time, Candi Borobudur has become the object of
Borobudur tour which attracts many tourists both local and foreign. . In addition, the
Borobudur temple has become a shrine for Buddhist in Indonesia and became the center of the
most important annual Buddhist namely Waisak.

Borobudur is a evidence into one of the grandeur and human ingenuity has ever made in
