100km in 48 hrs: OXFAM Trailwalker Brisbane

July 11, 2012 by Michael Branch

0



Another beautiful day in sunny Brisbane as the sun rose above the horizon and the blue sky began
it’s natural melody. Teams from all over the country had assembled near Mt Glorious just
west of Brisbane for the start of the annual OXFAM Trail walker, a one hundred kilometre hike
through some of most beautiful national parks in Australia ……

Saving lives by trekking miles

There were over 1200 participants in this year’s Brisbane OXFAM Trailwalker, all f which
raised much needed funds to help the less fortunate throughout the world . Over $750,000 was
raised for the renowned charity, assisting the sick, poor and injured throughout war torn
nations around the globe. Fishing nets can be bought for villages in East Timor , medical
supplies can now be sent to Uganda , running water can be provided for villages in Africa and
lives can be saved world wide. Well done Brisbane, you showed that true Aussie spirit of
helping those less fortunate.

Tackling the trail

I took the liberty of joining one of the teams on this year’s trail for the arduous 100km trek,
a chance to feel a little pain for the greater good. The trail summited no less than five
mountains throughout the D’Aguilar National Park , wound through beautiful valleys, slid
down slippery slopes and punched through cool water streams. An amazingly beautiful trek
that had to be seen to be experienced for its true nature. Whilst it may sound like a great task in
a scenic part of the world, this year’s OXFAM Trailwalker proved to be not for the faint
hearted. Many people and teams retired throughout the course and I am positive a lot more were
feeling the pain I too felt on this journey. A great effort for those who finished and a great
cause for all participants…..

Chase the dream

Many of us in the modern world today wish for a better place to live, a safer place to raise our
children, a world of peace, a world of charity and a world without pain. Unfortunately this may
never be a reality, but that does not mean we need to give up trying. For many of us the simple
liberties of freedom, running water, a steady income, ample food and access to medical
supplies is taken for granted. For many of us, we do not even think of these things as the seem
only natural to most of us to have access to these amenities. Unfortunately there are still
people throughout the world that consider these things luxuries, a world away, and death
comes far too early for the young in these areas. So I beg of the people such as myself out there,
that perhaps we open our eyes for a moment and look at what is really happening in this world,
look and you will see……

Be better than most…. Help a charity that can assist these people and be a better human
being….

Vision beyond the horizon is the path to success…..
