Squid Sandwich (Bocadillo de Calamares)



24 Saturday Mar 2012

Posted by acorninmykitchen in Recipes

≈ 5 Comments



Tags

Bread , Food , Main courses , Recipes , Starters , Tapas





Image:
http://acorninmykitchen.files.wordpress.com/2012/03/bocata.jpg?w=225&h=300

A simple one. Typical from Madrid and perfect with a cold beer. For me, a delicacy.

I am not sure if there is a proper translation in english for Bocadillo . If there is one please
let me know.



    Ingredients (for 2)

    - 2 clean squids.

    

    - 2 Baguette type bread.

    

    - Flour.

    

    - Salt.

    

    - Sunflower Oil.

    

    - A very cold beer (optional).

    



PreparationÂ 

Cut the squids in rings. Add salt. Bater them in flour. The easiest and cleanest way is to put the
flour and the squid rings in a tupper, close the lid and shake.

Put a pan with sunflower oil(around 300 ml.) at high heat. When the oil gets very hot, fry the
squid rings until golden and crispy. Place them in a plate with a paper kitchen towel to get rid
of the oil excess.

Open the bread. Place the squid rings and a few drops of lemon juice. Open your beer and feel in
